Problem Statement:
Australia’s housing affordability crisis is fundamentally demand-driven. Escalating population
growth fuelled largely by Net Overseas Migration has consistently outpaced dwelling supply,
pushing rents and prices beyond the reach of median households. According to the National
Housing Supply and Affordability Council (NHSAC) State of the Housing System 2025 report,
2024 saw 223,000 new households formed against only 177,000 completions, widening the
National Housing Accord shortfall to 262,000 units. This structural imbalance has resulted in a
national price-to-income ratio of 8.0, rental stress affecting more than 50% of lower-income
households, and growing pressure on frontline service providers such as The Salvation Army
(The Salvos), who manage thousands of tenancies and emergency housing placements each
year.
Without an early-warning mechanism that distinguishes migration-driven surges from natural
population growth, policymakers and NGOs remain reactive rather than proactive. This project
addresses that gap by developing the Housing Pressure Early Signal (HPES): a data-driven
indicator that quantifies when demand persistently outpaces supply.

Objectives-
The primary objective is to build and validate HPES, a simple yet powerful early-warning
indicator that compares population growth (demand) with dwelling approvals (supply) while
decomposing demand into its core drivers (Natural Increase vs Net Overseas Migration).
Specific goals include:
● Quantifying structural housing pressure using 40+ years of ABS data (1981–2025).
● Identifying migration as the dominant driver of recent HPES spikes.
● Providing actionable intelligence for NGOs like The Salvation Army to anticipate rental
stress 6–12 months in advance.
● Delivering a production-ready Streamlit dashboard and policy recommendations to help
close the National Housing Accord gap.
